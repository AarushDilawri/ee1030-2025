\textbf{Question:} \\
Show that $(-1,2,1), \; (1,-2,5), \; (4,-7,8)$ and $(2,-3,4)$ are the vertices of a parallelogram.

\bigskip
\textbf{Solution:} \\
Let
\begin{align}
	\vec{A} = \myvec{-1 \\ 2 \\ 1},
	\vec{B}= \myvec{1 \\ -2 \\ 5}, 
\vec{C} = \myvec{4 \\ -7 \\ 8},
\vec{D} = \myvec{2 \\ -3 \\ 4}.
\end{align}

Now,
\begin{align}
	\vec{B - A}= \myvec{1 - (-1) \\ -2 - 2 \\ 5 - 1}
      = \myvec{2 \\ -4 \\ 4}, \\[6pt]
	\vec{C - D} = \myvec{4 - 2 \\ -7 - (-3) \\ 8 - 4}
      = \myvec{2 \\ -4 \\ 4}.
\end{align}

Hence,
\begin{align}
	\vec{B - A} = \vec{C - D}= \myvec{2 \\ -4 \\ 4}.
\end{align}
Hence,                                              
\[ \vec{B - A} ||\vec{C - D }\] 

Also,
\begin{align}
	\vec{C - B}= \myvec{4 - 1 \\ -7 - (-2) \\ 8 - 5}
      = \myvec{3 \\ -5 \\ 3}, \\[6pt]
	\vec{D - A }= \myvec{2 - (-1) \\ -3 - 2 \\ 4 - 1}
      = \myvec{3 \\ -5 \\ 3}.
\end{align}

Thus,
\begin{align}
	\vec{C - B}= \vec{D - A} = \myvec{3 \\ -5 \\ 3}.
\end{align}
Hence,
\[ \vec{C - B} ||\vec{D - A }\] 
\bigskip
\noindent
Therefore, $A, B, C, D$ are the vertices of a parallelogram.

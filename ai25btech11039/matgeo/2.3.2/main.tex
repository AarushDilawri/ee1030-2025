\section*{Question}
\textbf{Q.}\; Find the angle between unit vectors $\vec a$ and $\vec b$ such that $\sqrt{3}\,\vec a-\vec b$ is also a unit vector.


\section*{Solution}
Let
\[
\vec a=\myvec{a_1\\a_2\\a_3},\qquad
\vec b=\myvec{b_1\\b_2\\b_3},\qquad
\vec a^{\top}\vec a=\vec b^{\top}\vec b=1.
\]
If $\sqrt{3}\,\vec a-\vec b$ is a unit vector, then
\[
1=\|\sqrt{3}\,\vec a-\vec b\|^2
=(\sqrt{3}\,\vec a-\vec b)^{\top}(\sqrt{3}\,\vec a-\vec b)
=3\,\vec a^{\top}\vec a+\vec b^{\top}\vec b-2\sqrt{3}\,\vec a^{\top}\vec b.
\]
Hence
\[
4-2\sqrt{3}\,(\vec a^{\top}\vec b)=1
\;\Longrightarrow\;
\vec a^{\top}\vec b=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}.
\]
Let $\theta$ be the angle between $\vec a$ and $\vec b$;
$\cos\theta=\vec a^{\top}\vec b$, so
\[
\cos\theta=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\quad\Rightarrow\quad
\boxed{\theta=30^\circ}.
\]

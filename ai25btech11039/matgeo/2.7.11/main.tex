\section*{Question}
\textbf{Q 2.7.11.} Find the area of the triangle with vertices
$A=\myvec{1\\-1}$,\; $B=\myvec{-4\\6}$,\; $C=\myvec{-3\\-5}$.


\section*{Solution}
Let the vertices be
\[
\vec{A} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}, \quad 
\vec{B} = \begin{bmatrix} -4 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix}, \quad 
\vec{C} = \begin{bmatrix} -3 \\ -5 \end{bmatrix}.
\]

\medskip

The area of the triangle is
\[
\Delta = \frac{1}{2}\Big|\det\Big((\vec{B}-\vec{A}), (\vec{C}-\vec{A})\Big)\Big|.
\]

\medskip

Now,
\[
\vec{B}-\vec{A} = \begin{bmatrix} -4 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}
= \begin{bmatrix} -5 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix},
\]
\[
\vec{C}-\vec{A} = \begin{bmatrix} -3 \\ -5 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}
= \begin{bmatrix} -4 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix}.
\]

\medskip

So,
\[
\det\Big((\vec{B}-\vec{A}), (\vec{C}-\vec{A})\Big) 
= \begin{vmatrix} -5 & -4 \\ 7 & -4 \end{vmatrix}
= (-5)(-4) - (7)(-4) = 20 + 28 = 48.
\]

\medskip

Hence,
\[
\Delta = \frac{1}{2} \times |48| = 24.
\]

\medskip

\textbf{Final Answer: } The area of the triangle is
\[
\Delta = 24.
\]

\textbf{Question}:\\
\text {If three points } (x, -1),\ (2, 1)\ \text{and}\ (4, 5)\ \text{are collinear, find the value of}\ x.
\\
\textbf{Solution: }

\[
\begin{align}
\Rightarrow
\begin{pmatrix}
a - b & a - c \\
\end{pmatrix}
\]
\end{align}
\[
\begin{pmatrix}
x - 2 & x - 4 \\
-1-1 & -1-5
\end{pmatrix}
\Rightarrow
\begin{pmatrix}
x - 2 & x - 4 \\
-2 & -6
\end{pmatrix}
\]

Changing the matrix into row echelon form, 
using row operations,

\[
R_2 \leftrightarrow R_1
\Rightarrow
\begin{pmatrix}
-2 & -6 \\
x - 2 & x - 4
\end{pmatrix}
\]

Again,

\[
R_2 \rightarrow R_2 + \left(\frac{x - 2}{2}\right) R_1
\]

\[
\begin{pmatrix}
-2 & -6 \\
0 & x - 4 + 6 - 3x
\end{pmatrix}
=
\begin{pmatrix}
-2 & -6 \\
0 & -2x + 2
\end{pmatrix}
\]

As the condition for three points to be collinear, Rank of the Matrix should be 1

\[
-2x + 2 = 0
\Rightarrow x = 1
\]
Hence, the value of x  is '1'.
